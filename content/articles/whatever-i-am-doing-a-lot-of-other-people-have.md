Are you Living in a Cave?
</a></aside>

> Too many startup people act like they are sitting in a cave inventing
> something utterly new.
> <br><br>
>  Chances are, they’re not.
> <br><br>
>  I constantly find myself amazed when I recommend a blog post, or a
> tactic to someone and they are like “Oh, that’s just what I need! I
> never thought to search for that.” Even more amazing is when someone
> labors day and night trying to figure out how to make a successful app
> yet has never heard of something like a/b testing.
> <br><br>
>  I feel like these people must be living in caves.
> <br><br>
>  One of the fun parts of the University of Chicago was that we
> constantly had to do research. This, plus being a programmer, entirely
> convinced me of the following:
> <br><br>
>  “Whatever I am doing, a lot of other people have probably also tried
> to do it. Some of them have probably been way more successful than I
> have been so far. Some of them have probably written books or blog
> posts about how they successfully did what I am doing now. I can find
> these books and blog posts on Google and then apply what they say.”
> <br><br>
>  The four sentences above are somehow not common knowledge, in my
> experience. I have no idea why.
> <br><br>

Google, APIs, books, blogs, and most importantly *other people* are my
external memory and processing units. We are all part of the same
beowulf cluster! Use the fantastic abilities of the other nodes around
you!

# OSX: 5 second timeout for domains defined in /etc/hosts


<p><em>This is mostly for my own reference, this document has been sitting in my drafts folder since &hellip; like two years ago.</em></p>

<p>First of all, OSX claims <code>.local</code> for Bonjour and will treat them differently, so use <code>.dev</code> or something instead.</p>

<p>To add a host called <code>myapp.dev</code> and point it to <code>localhost</code>, just do this:</p>

<pre><code>sudo dscl localhost -create /Local/Default/Hosts/mydev.dev IPAddress 127.0.0.1
</code></pre>

<p>To see all the currently defined hosts and their IPs:</p>

<pre><code>sudo dscl localhost -list /Local/Default/Hosts IPAddress
</code></pre>

<p>And to remove a host:</p>

<pre><code>sudo dscl localhost -delete /Local/Default/Hosts/mydev.dev
</code></pre>

<p>if you declare both a IPv4 and IPv6 address in <code>/etc/hosts</code> for a <code>.dev</code> domain like so:</p>

<pre><code>27.0.0.1 myapp.dev
::1 myapp.dev
</code></pre>

<p>Then it <em>should</em> work correctly.</p>
